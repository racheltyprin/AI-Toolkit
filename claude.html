30-Minute Workflows

Detailed step-by-step guides for each workflow. Follow along to build your first prototype.

üí° First Time Here?

Start with Workflow #3 (AI-Enhanced Design Sprint). It requires zero setup and teaches you the core pattern: use AI to generate content, refine it with design tools, and publish something real.

Workflow #1: Non-Technical MVP Builder

Build a functional app without code. Total time: 40 minutes.

Step 1: Generate Your App (15 min)

Go to Lovable.dev or Bolt.new. Describe your app idea in detail: "Create a habit tracker where users can add daily goals, mark them complete, and see a weekly progress chart."

Pro tip: Be specific about features but flexible about design. The AI will generate a working prototype you can see immediately.

Step 2: Refine the Interface (10 min)

Export your design or take screenshots. Open Figma (free account) and recreate the key screens. This helps you think through user flow and make intentional design decisions.

Step 3: Build Data Structure (10 min)

Open Glide or Softr. Create a simple spreadsheet or Airtable base with your data structure. Connect it to your app template. Now you have a working prototype with real data.

Step 4: Share & Test (5 min)

Get a shareable link. Send it to 3-5 people. Watch them use it (screen share if possible). Take notes on where they get confused.

Workflow #2: Research to Demo Creator

Transform research into an interactive demo. Total time: 35 minutes.

Step 1: Research Your Space (10 min)

Open Perplexity. Ask: "What are the top 5 competitors in [your space]?" and "What features do users complain about in [competitor name]?" Take notes on gaps and opportunities.

Step 2: Sketch Your Concept (10 min)

Open Uizard or Figma. In Uizard, describe your app concept and let AI generate screens. In Figma, use a template from the community and customize it with your research insights.

Step 3: Build Interactive Prototype (10 min)

In Framer, describe your concept: "A task management app focused on [your unique angle from research]." Generate the site, then customize with your specific features and copy.

Step 4: Polish & Share (5 min)

Add realistic content (not lorem ipsum). Get a shareable link. Test it on mobile and desktop before sending to users.

Workflow #3: AI-Enhanced Design Sprint

Create a complete landing page. Total time: 30 minutes.

Step 1: Generate Copy (8 min)

Tell ChatGPT: "I'm building [your product]. Write a landing page with headline, 3 benefit sections, and a call to action. Make it conversational and specific, not generic marketing speak."

Step 2: Create Visuals (10 min)

In Canva, search for landing page templates. Pick one, customize with your copy. Use DALL‚Ä¢E (in ChatGPT) to generate 2-3 custom images: "A clean, modern illustration of [your concept]."

Step 3: Build the Site (10 min)

Go to Carrd or Framer. In Carrd, pick a template and paste your sections. In Framer, describe your site and let AI generate it, then paste in your refined copy.

Step 4: Publish & Test (2 min)

Click publish. Share the link. Send to 10 people and ask: "Would you click the call-to-action button? Why or why not?"

üéØ Common Patterns Across All Workflows

  • Start with AI generation to get 80% there fast

  • Test immediately with real people
  • Iterate based on feedback, not assumptions

Website Building

Framer

Freemium

Describe the site you want and Framer generates a polished, responsive website you can edit and publish instantly. Its biggest strength isn't just AI‚Äîit's the editor itself. It feels like Figma but with real production output, making it one of the smoothest design-to-live workflows available.

When to use Framer

Choose Framer when you need a beautiful, interactive website fast and want the flexibility to customize every detail. Perfect for portfolios, landing pages, and marketing sites.

Carrd

Freemium

A simple, fast website builder for landing pages, portfolios, and one-page sites. Carrd is lightweight, affordable, and surprisingly flexible‚Äîperfect for quick launches when you don't need a full CMS.

When to use Carrd

Use Carrd for simple one-pagers, link-in-bio sites, or when you need something live in 15 minutes. Great for event pages, personal sites, and quick experiments.

Lovable

Freemium

Prompt-to-app platform that generates full web apps using React and Supabase. Great for MVPs and quick product experiments, with solid GitHub integration. Pricing limits can be restrictive as projects scale.

When to use Lovable

Choose Lovable when you need a functional web app (not just a website) with user authentication, databases, or interactive features. Better for products than marketing sites.

App Building

Glide

Freemium

Turns spreadsheets or databases into polished mobile and web apps without coding. Great for internal tools, client portals, and simple SaaS ideas with fast setup and clean UI.

When to use Glide

Perfect for data-heavy apps, inventory systems, CRMs, or any tool where the core functionality is viewing and editing structured data. Works best when your data already exists in sheets.

Softr

Freemium

Build web apps and client portals on top of Airtable or databases. Easy to use, good templates, and ideal for marketplaces, dashboards, and membership sites without heavy development.

When to use Softr

Choose Softr for directory sites, member portals, or marketplaces. Especially good when you're using Airtable as your backend and need a polished front-end quickly.

Microsoft Power Platform

Enterprise

Includes Power Apps (app building), Power Automate (workflow automation), and Power BI (analytics). Deep integration with Microsoft tools and AI Builder lets you add custom AI capabilities without coding‚Äîespecially powerful for organizations already using the Microsoft ecosystem.

When to use Power Platform

Best for enterprise environments or when you need tight integration with Microsoft 365, SharePoint, or Teams. Free for students through most university licenses.

From Prototype to User Testing

You've built something. Now what? Here's how to get it in front of users and learn fast.

üéØ The Goal

Don't aim for perfection. Aim to learn whether your core assumption is true. Every prototype should answer one key question about your users' needs.

Step 1: Define Your Test Question

What You're Really Testing

Before showing anyone, write down: "I believe [specific user type] will [take specific action] because [assumption about their need]."

Example: "I believe busy parents will save recipes they find on Instagram because they're frustrated forgetting great content they scroll past."

Step 2: Recruit 5 Test Users

Friends & Family (The Easy Path)

Pros: Available immediately, free, honest feedback. Cons: May not be your target user, might be too polite.

Make it work: Give them a specific task and watch them‚Äîdon't explain anything. Silence reveals problems.

Stanford Community

Post in department Slack channels, student Facebook groups, or relevant email lists. Offer coffee gift cards ($5-10) for 15 minutes of feedback.

User Testing Platforms

UserTesting.com: Pay $49+ per tester for video walkthroughs. Lyssna: Cheaper alternative for basic feedback.

Step 3: Run the Test Session

The 15-Minute Test Script

Minute 0-2: "Thanks for helping. I'm testing a prototype. Think out loud as you use it. There are no wrong answers‚Äîany confusion is my fault, not yours."

Minute 2-10: Give them a realistic task. Watch and take notes. Don't help unless they're completely stuck.

Minute 10-13: Ask follow-up questions about confusion points.

Minute 13-15: "Would you use this if it existed? Would you pay for it? Who else would benefit?"

Step 4: Analyze Patterns

What to Look For

  • Where do ALL 5 users get confused? ‚Üí Critical fix needed
  • What do 3+ users misunderstand? ‚Üí Major redesign required
  • What do users ask for that you didn't build? ‚Üí Consider for V2
  • What features do users ignore completely? ‚Üí Probably cut them

Step 5: Iterate Fast

Quick Fixes (Do Immediately)

Confusing copy, broken flows, obvious UI issues. Make changes and test again with 2-3 new users within 48 hours.

Medium Changes (This Week)

Restructuring navigation, adding missing features, redesigning confused sections. Test again before moving forward.

Big Pivots (Pause & Reflect)

If 4+ users don't understand the core value, or no one would actually use it‚Äîyou might need to rethink the concept, not just the prototype.

üöÄ Ready to Ship?

Once 4 out of 5 users successfully complete your core task without help, and at least 3 say they'd actually use it‚Äîyou're ready to build a real V1 or start showing it to potential customers.
